## Context
Service reliability was acceptable in normal operation, but teams had limited confidence in behavior under partial failures and dependency outages.

## Problem
Incident response was reactive. Failure scenarios were discussed but rarely practiced in realistic environments.

## Constraints
- Experiments had to be safe and reversible.
- Customer impact risk had to stay near zero.
- Team trust in the process had to be built gradually.

## Solution
I introduced lightweight chaos experiments:
- selected non-destructive failure scenarios first,
- tested dependency latency and service restarts in controlled windows,
- documented expected behavior and recovery signals before each test,
- converted findings into backlog items with owners and deadlines.

## Tools
- Kubernetes
- Observability dashboards
- Alerting workflows
- Runbooks

## Result
Teams improved confidence in failure handling, and platform work contributed to a **20% downtime reduction** over the following cycles.

## Lessons Learned
Chaos engineering is most effective when framed as learning, not stress testing. Clear hypotheses and follow-through on fixes are essential.
