## Context
Our platform footprint grew quickly over two years. Engineering velocity was strong, but infrastructure spend increased faster than product usage.

## Problem
Monthly cloud costs were climbing without clear ownership. Teams could not confidently explain where spend came from or which workloads were driving the trend.

## Constraints
- No production downtime during optimization.
- No aggressive rightsizing that could increase incident risk.
- Savings had to be sustainable, not one-time cuts.

## Solution
I introduced a staged FinOps workflow:
- tagged all critical workloads for clear allocation,
- created weekly cost reports by service and environment,
- rightsized underutilized compute,
- moved bursty workloads to schedule-based scaling,
- reduced log retention where compliance allowed,
- migrated selected storage classes for infrequently accessed data.

## Tools
- AWS Cost Explorer
- AWS Compute Optimizer
- CloudWatch
- Terraform
- Internal cost dashboards

## Result
The environment reached **35% overall cloud cost reduction**, equivalent to approximately **$75k annual savings**, while maintaining service performance and deployment speed.

## Lessons Learned
Cost optimization works best when treated as an engineering routine, not a finance-only activity. Clear ownership, simple dashboards, and weekly reviews prevent drift.
